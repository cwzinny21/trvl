Greece is a country located in the South of Europe and borders four different seas: the Aegean, Cretan, Libyan, and Ionian. The main language spoken is Greek and the currency used there is the Euro.

Athens, Santorini, Mykonos, Ios, Crete

visit the acropolis, go out on a boat, swim in the Aegean Sea, go snorkeling, jump off of cliffs, shop in the quaint, charming towns

Italy is a country located in South Central Europe surrounded by the Medditeranean Sea. The native language is Italian and the used currency is the Euro.

Rome, Venice, Florence, Amalfi Coast

visit historical sites such as the Colosseum, the Leaning Tower of Pisa, Pompeii, drive along the Amalfi coast, ride a gondola in Venice, explore the rich cultures of each city.

lots of pizza, pasta, meat, tomatoes, salads, and olive oil

Argentina is a country in the southern half of South America. The main currency used is the Argentine Peso and the primary language is Spanish.

Buenos Aires, Cordoba, Mendoza, Ushuaia

visit the many nature sites, walk the streets or Buenos Aires, go whale watching, visit the restaurants and immerse yourself in the culture and food of Argentina.

influenced by Medditerrean and Indigenous cultures, the food consists of lots of meat, such as ribs, steak, beef, chicken, and chorizo.

Peru is a country located in western South America. The main language spoken there is Spanish and the official currency is the Peruvian Sol.

Lima, Cuzco, Trujillo, Arequipa, Nazca

hike Machu Picchu, explore the Amazon, climb the various mountains, walk the streets of Lima, take a river cruise along the waters of the Amazon.

a unique, cultural blend of indigenous and European influence, some staples being corn, potatoes, legumes, rice, wheat, and meat.

Tokyo, Kyoto, Osaka, Takayama, Fukuoka

explore the city of Tokyo, visit the Arashiyama Bamboo Forest, watch a sumo-wrestling match, ride the bullet train through the mountains.

consisting of rice, miso soup, seafood (fish, often used in sushi), vegetables, noodles.
